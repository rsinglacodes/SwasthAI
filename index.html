Healthcare Awareness Vaccines & Milestones

💉0–6 weeks
BCG & Hepatitis B Vaccine
BCG protects against tuberculosis. Hepatitis B first dose should be given at birth. Both are part of the national immunisation schedule.
🔴 Mandatory at birth
💉6 weeks
DTP & Polio (OPV/IPV)
Diphtheria, Tetanus, and Pertussis vaccine. Oral or injectable polio vaccine. Given at 6, 10, and 14 weeks.
💉9 months
Measles & Vitamin A
First dose of measles vaccine at 9 months. Vitamin A supplementation begins at 6 months to prevent deficiency and blindness.
💉By age 2
MMR Vaccine
Measles, Mumps, and Rubella — second dose at 15–18 months. Essential to prevent highly contagious childhood diseases.
🔴 Do not skip booster
🦷Age 5–7
First Dental Check-up
Children should have their first full dental examination before starting school. Early cavities can affect speech and nutrition.
💉By age 10
Typhoid & Hepatitis A
Typhoid conjugate vaccine (TCV) should be given by age 9–10. Hepatitis A vaccine recommended for children in high-risk areas.
💉Age 11–13
HPV Vaccine
Human Papillomavirus vaccine prevents cervical cancer and genital warts. Best given before sexual activity begins. 2 doses for ages 9–14.
⚡ High priority for girls
🧠Age 13–17
Mental Health Screening
Adolescence is a critical window for mental health. Annual screening for depression and anxiety is recommended by age 13.
💉Every year
Annual Influenza Vaccine
Flu vaccines should be taken every year as strains change. Especially important for adults with chronic conditions, healthcare workers, and pregnant women.
🩺Age 30+
Annual Full Body Check-up
Blood pressure, blood sugar, cholesterol, kidney function, and liver panel tests are essential after 30. Early detection saves lives.
⚡ Especially if diabetic
💉Every 10 years
Td Booster (Tetanus)
Tetanus and diphtheria booster should be repeated every 10 years. Also recommended after deep wounds or animal bites.
💉Age 60+
Pneumococcal Vaccine
Protects against pneumonia, meningitis, and bloodstream infections. A single dose is usually sufficient for healthy older adults.
🔴 High risk group
👁️Age 60+
Eye & Hearing Tests
Annual vision and hearing check-ups are critical for seniors. Untreated vision loss is a leading cause of falls and accidents.
🚰All ages
Safe Water & Hygiene
Drink only clean, filtered, or boiled water. Wash hands before meals and after using the toilet. Prevents 80% of common infections.
🌞All ages
Vitamin D & Sunlight
15–20 minutes of morning sunlight daily boosts Vitamin D. Deficiency is linked to depression, weak immunity, and bone disease.
